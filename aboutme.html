My First Design Process

Designer with an Engineering Degree

Engineering is Problem Solving

   I came to college thinking I would graduate an engineer, but instead will be graduating as a user-oriented interaction designer with a non-traditional engineering education. After being exposed to the user-oriented design process during the summer after my first year, I realized that this passion for tackling challenges was going to be best utilized as a designer, where I can think critically about the experience of the user and create experiences that are tailored to the user’s needs.

Not First World Problems, real World Problems

   As I continue to develop as a designer, I want to ensure that my knowledge and efforts are working towards a better world. I stumbled upon this medium article by Marco Marandiz which discusses Silicon Valley’s vast level of expertise which is wasted on creating apps what solve first world problems. I don’t want to solve first world problems; I want to solve real World Problems. I want to reduce human suffering and inefficiency by creating interactions that are free of confusion and enjoyable to use.

Designing for Users and Non-Users Alike

   Even though most of my work is supported by discovered needs of users, it is also important to consider the non-users. It is the job of creators to ensure that the products we create are not just beneficial for our users, but for humanity. I aim to not lose sight of the bigger picture as I jump from project to project, and wrestle with ecological and moral considerations as I work.
